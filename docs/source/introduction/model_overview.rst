Model Overview
==============

GCAM-Europe is built on a modular, sectorally disaggregated structure derived from the GCAM
framework. The model is developed as a set of interconnected components representing key systems,
including energy, land use, water resources, and the economy.

This modular design allows for flexible configuration of the model and enables analysis at both
sectoral and system-wide levels.


Sectoral structure
-------------------

The model is organised into multiple interacting sectors, each developed with a degree of modularity
that allows for isolated testing and analysis when required. The main sectoral components include:

- Energy supply and transformation
- Electricity generation and transmission
- Residential and commercial buildings
- Industry
- Transport
- Land use and agriculture
- Water resources


Model integration and coupling
-------------------------------

Although sectoral modules can be analysed independently, GCAM-Europe is designed as an integrated
assessment model in which all components are interlinked.

Key interactions include:

- Energy–economy linkages through demand for energy services
- Land–energy interactions through bioenergy production
- Water–energy interactions via cooling, hydropower, and agriculture
- Climate feedbacks affecting resource availability and productivity

This integrated structure allows the model to capture system-wide trade-offs and synergies across
sectors under different policy scenarios.


Spatial and temporal resolution
-------------------------------

The model operates at high spatial resolution for Europe, where each of the 39 countries is represented
as an individual region. The rest of the world is represented using the original GCAM regional structure.

Temporal dynamics are represented in five-year time steps from 1990 to 2100. This allows the model to
capture long-term structural transitions in energy systems, land use, and emissions pathways.


Model use and applications
--------------------------

GCAM-Europe is designed to support:

- Analysis of European climate and energy policy scenarios
- Assessment of national and EU-wide mitigation strategies
- Evaluation of cross-border spillover effects (e.g., carbon leakage)
- Exploration of long-term technology transition pathways

The model can be used both as a fully integrated IAM and in a more modular fashion, depending on the
research question and computational constraints.
