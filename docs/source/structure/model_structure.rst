Model Structure
===============

The GCAM-Europe model follows a modular integrated assessment structure derived from the GCAM framework.
This section provides a high-level overview of the main components of the model. Detailed descriptions
are provided in the subsequent subsections.


Overview of model components
----------------------------

GCAM-Europe is organised into several interconnected modules representing key socio-economic and
environmental systems.


Energy System
-------------

The energy system represents primary energy supply, conversion technologies, and final energy use
across all sectors.

- Primary energy resources  
- Electricity generation  
- Transmission and distribution  
- Final energy demand  


Buildings Sector
----------------

The buildings module represents residential and commercial energy demand.

- Space heating and cooling  
- Water heating  
- Cooking  
- Appliance electricity demand  


Industry Sector
---------------

The industry sector represents industrial production and energy use.

- Energy-intensive industries  
- Process heat demand  
- Industrial fuel switching  


Transport Sector
----------------

The transport module covers passenger and freight transport.

- Road transport  
- Rail transport  
- Aviation  
- Shipping  


Electricity System
------------------

The electricity system represents generation, dispatch, and grid representation.

- Power generation technologies  
- Grid regions  
- Load segments  
- Storage technologies  


Land Use System
---------------

The land module represents land allocation and agricultural production.

- Cropland allocation  
- Forestry  
- Livestock systems  
- Bioenergy production  


Water System
------------

The water module represents water supply and demand across sectors.

- Water abstraction  
- Agricultural water use  
- Energy-water interactions  
- Hydrological constraints  


Climate System
--------------

The climate module represents greenhouse gas emissions and climate feedbacks.

- GHG emissions accounting  
- Radiative forcing  
- Climate impacts linkages  


Regional Structure
------------------

GCAM-Europe represents Europe at country-level resolution.

- 39 European countries as individual regions  
- Rest of the world based on standard GCAM aggregation  
- Trade and interaction between regions  


Temporal Resolution
-------------------

The model runs over a long-term horizon with discrete time steps.

- Simulation period: 1990–2100  
- Time step: 5 years  
- Scenario-based projections  


Model Outputs
-------------

GCAM-Europe produces a wide range of integrated outputs.

- Energy system trajectories  
- Land-use changes  
- Water use and availability  
- Emissions and climate indicators
